Skyquire

Dec 19 Launch Scope — what's in, what waits

The locked cut for go-live. v1 is everything that must work on launch day; v1.x ships within weeks and is announced as "coming"; v2 is the post-launch quarter. The discipline: cut anything regulated-and-slow, platform-slow, or supply-irrelevant — none of it blocks first listings or first matched deals.
Verticals: Both — SMB + Corporate M&A, equal weight Surface: Web + native mobile (iOS & Android), both polished Pricing: Free at launch — seed supply first Ops: Manual / mediated, admin console web-only

IN V1 Must work Dec 19

Public launch · web + native mobile
🛒SMB marketplace — browse, filter, listing detail, buyer onboarding & profile
🏢Corporate M&A suite — target discovery, deal pipeline, deal rooms, valuation, DD assistant
💬Inquiries + mediated messaging — buyer↔team↔seller relay, confidentiality preserved
🏷️Seller platform — submit → verify → publish pipeline (your supply funnel)
🤖AI that's already solid — health score, Aria + pgvector matching, smart search
🛡️Manual trust layer — you verify each listing & mediate each deal (a feature at launch volume)
📄Click-wrap NDA + ToS gating contact; DPDP-compliant privacy & consent
📱Native mobile (Expo/RN) — consumer flows: browse, match, inquire, chat, submit, status, notifications
🚀Launch surface — landing, pricing page, demo video, waitlist conversion

🤝Investor round — pre-seed deck + conversations
⚙️Scale ops — automate parts of verification once volume demands it

Why this cut

Free at launch keeps Razorpay KYC and Apple/Google in-app-billing rules off the critical path, and free/founder pricing is the fastest way to seed the supply a marketplace needs to be worth anything.

Escrow + eSign are deliberately v2 — both are regulated vendor integrations whose timelines you don't control; nothing about them blocks a first listing or a first matched deal.

Admin console stays web-only — you run verification & mediation from a laptop. Mobile mirrors the consumer surface, not the whole platform — that's what keeps "perfect mobile" achievable solo.

The honest risk: two full verticals across web and two native apps, solo, is the most ambitious version. It survives only on sequencing — web + backend feature-stable by the September gate so Oct–Nov is mobile build + polish, hitting a mid-November app-store freeze with buffer. And it doubles the cold-start problem: you must seed two kinds of inventory (SMBs and corporate mandates). Supply, not code, is the make-or-break.
