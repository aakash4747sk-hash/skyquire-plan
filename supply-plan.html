② Supply playbook — channels, ranked by leverage

🤝 CA / advisor / broker partnerships Highest leverage

CAs and business brokers already sit on a pipeline of owners who want to exit. One good CA = many listings.
  • → Target practising CAs, company secretaries, GST consultants who serve SMBs in your launch cities (start: your own city + 2 metros).
  • → Offer: list your clients' businesses free, we handle the buyer side + verification; you stay the trusted advisor and earn a referral/success fee.
  • → Existing business brokers: co-list their inventory; you bring tech + buyers, they bring listings.
  • → Tap ICAI study circles, local CA associations, MSME consultants — one talk/webinar = 20 warm intros.

📞 Founder-led direct outbound Fastest start

The unscalable engine that gets you from 0→20. You personally find and pitch sellers.
  • → Build a hit-list of businesses likely to sell: owners 55+, "business for sale" posts, distressed-but-profitable, franchise resales, founders posting about burnout/exit.
  • → Sources: IndiaMART, JustDial, Flippa/SMERGERS/IndiaBizForSale listings, franchise resale boards, LinkedIn, local trade directories.
  • → Reach out 1:1 (call/WhatsApp/email). Lead with "I may have a serious, verified buyer for your business" — not "join my platform."
  • → Concierge-list for them: you fill the listing, verify docs, publish. Zero friction for the seller.

🗂️ Aggregate existing listings Fills the shelf fast

Don't launch empty. Seed the marketplace with real (permissioned) inventory so it looks alive.
  • → Identify businesses already publicly for sale on other portals; reach the owner/broker and offer to cross-list with verification (a quality upgrade for them).
  • → Only list with explicit permission — never scrape-and-publish. Verification is your differentiator; protect it.
  • → Prioritise variety (sector + price band) over volume, to hit "minimum viable liquidity".

👥 Communities & associations Compounding

Go where SMB owners and acquirers already gather.
  • → MSME associations, trade bodies, industry clusters (textile, F&B, auto-parts hubs), local chambers of commerce.
  • → Founder/operator communities (Headstart, TiE chapters, OPN, sector WhatsApp/Telegram groups, SaaS/D2C founder circles).
  • → Your own two WhatsApp communities (Buyers / Sellers) from the content plan — seed, then nurture into listings.
  • → Inbound from the Marketing Road Path (waitlist, "sell your business" posts) feeds here.

⑦ Honest risks & how to de-risk
  • ⚠️ Two-sided doubles the work. Sourcing SMB sellers AND corporate mandates AND buyers at once is a lot for a solo founder. De-risk: go SMB-supply-first — it's the harder, slower side; corporate mandates can lag a few weeks.
  • ⚠️ Sellers are slow & secretive. Exits are emotional and confidential; conversations take weeks. De-risk: start NOW, keep a fat pipeline, and let confidentiality (anonymity until they choose) be the pitch.
  • ⚠️ Verification is a bottleneck. Each listing needs real document checks. De-risk: tighten the verification checklist, batch it, and use CA partners who can vouch for clients' books.
  • ⚠️ Empty-looking launch. <30 varied listings = dead marketplace. De-risk: aggregate permissioned inventory to backfill variety before going loud.
  • ⚠️ SEBI/advisory grey area (see Legal track). Confirm the model is clean before paying referral fees to CAs at scale.
