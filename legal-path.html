② The regulatory questions to resolve (with your lawyer)
1. Does running a business-sale / M&A marketplace trigger SEBI licensing?
For private SMB sales (selling a business as a going concern, or shares of an unlisted private company), this generally isn't dealing in "securities" the way listed-market broking is — so SEBI merchant-banker/broker rules typically don't apply. But: if you give deal-specific investment advice for a fee, the SEBI (Investment Advisers) Regulations could be implicated; and larger/structured equity deals get more sensitive. Action: get a written opinion from a securities lawyer on (a) marketplace vs adviser framing, (b) whether your fee model is "advisory," (c) any thresholds to stay under. Lean into "marketplace + tools," not "advice."
2. Can you charge success / referral fees — and pay CAs referral fees?
Brokerage/finder fees on private business sales are common, but the structure matters (who pays, when, GST treatment, whether it reads as regulated "advice"). Paying CA partners referral fees (core to the supply plan) must be a clean, documented arrangement. Action: have the lawyer/CA design the fee + referral structure before you sign CA partners at scale.
3. Handling money / escrow
Do not take custody of deal funds yourself — that invites payment-system/RBI exposure. Action: route any money through a regulated escrow provider (post-launch). Until then, you only facilitate; funds move directly between parties.
4. Data protection (DPDP Act 2023)
You collect PII + sensitive financials from both sides. The Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 requires lawful consent, purpose limitation, security safeguards, and a grievance/contact mechanism. Action: publish a compliant privacy policy, build consent into signup/listing, and document your data-handling. (Your RLS + verification model already help.)
5. Cross-border (FEMA) — only if relevant
If a buyer or seller is non-resident, FEMA rules on foreign investment/repatriation apply. Action: keep v1 domestic-only; revisit before enabling cross-border deals.
